Lesson 7: Parts of Speech

The Building Blocks of Every Sentence

Wow I really love creative writing and it helps me communicate effectively

Every word has a job to do in a sentence!

The 8 Parts of Speech

📦

1. NOUNS

Person, place, thing, or idea

Common nouns: phone, email, meeting, idea

Proper nouns: Google, Microsoft, LinkedIn, iPhone

Abstract nouns: happiness, freedom, success, love

Text Message:

"cant find my phone need to get to the store"

Professional Email:

"I need to purchase a new laptop for the presentation next week."

👤

2. PRONOUNS

Takes the place of a noun

Personal: I, you, he, she, it, we, they

Possessive: my, your, his, her, its, our, their

Demonstrative: this, that, these, those

Relative: who, whom, whose, which, that

Common Mistake:

"Me and John will handle the project."

Correct:

John and I will handle the project.

Pro tip: Remove "John" and see if it still makes sense. "Me will handle" is wrong, but "I will handle" is correct!

Social Media Fail:

"Your the best!"

"Its been a great day"

Fixed:

You're the best! (You are)

It's been a great day (It has)

⚡

3. VERBS

Action or state of being

Action verbs: run, write, email, tweet, post, message

Linking verbs: am, is, are, was, were, be, being, been

Helping verbs: can, could, will, would, shall, should, may, might, must

Text Confusion:

"The meeting starting soon"

Clear Communication:

The meeting is starting soon. (helping verb + action verb)

🎨

4. ADJECTIVES

Describes or modifies a noun

Quality: amazing, terrible, beautiful, ugly

Quantity: many, few, several, some

Size: big, small, huge, tiny

Color: red, blue, bright, dark

Plain:

"Had a day at work"

With adjectives:

"Had a challenging day at productive work"

Professional Examples:

"We need a comprehensive solution."

"The quarterly report shows impressive results."

🚀

5. ADVERBS

Modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs (often ends in -ly)

How: quickly, slowly, carefully, easily

When: now, later, soon, yesterday

Where: here, there, everywhere, outside

To what extent: very, quite, too, extremely

Weak Writing:

"I worked on the project."

Stronger with Adverbs:

"I carefully worked on the project yesterday."

Caution: Don't overuse adverbs! "Very, really, extremely" can weaken your writing. Instead of "very big," try "enormous" or "massive."
🔗

6. PREPOSITIONS

Shows relationship between nouns (location, time, direction)

Location: in, on, at, under, over, between, among

Time: before, after, during, since, until

Direction: to, from, toward, through, across

Email Examples:

"The meeting is at 3pm."

"I'll send it by Friday."

"Place the files on the server."

🔀

7. CONJUNCTIONS

Connects words, phrases, or clauses

Coordinating (FANBOYS): for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so

Subordinating: because, although, while, if, when, since

Correlative: both/and, either/or, neither/nor, not only/but also

Run-on Text:

"I sent the email I didn't get a reply I'm sending it again"

Fixed with Conjunctions:

"I sent the email, but I didn't get a reply, so I'm sending it again."

💥

8. INTERJECTIONS

Express emotion or exclamation

Common: wow, oh, ouch, hey, yay, ugh, hmm, oops

Too Casual for Work Email:

"OMG I totally forgot about the meeting!!!"

Professional Alternative:

"I apologize—I completely forgot about the meeting. Can we reschedule?"

Save interjections for casual texts, not professional emails!

Putting It All Together

Hey, I quickly finished that urgent report and sent it to the team

Part of Speech Word(s) Function
Interjection Hey Gets attention
Pronoun I, it Replaces nouns
Adverb quickly Describes how the action was done
Verb finished, sent Shows action
Adjective that, urgent, the Describes nouns
Noun report, team Names things
Conjunction and Connects ideas
Preposition to Shows relationship

Practice Exercise

Identify the part of speech for each underlined word:

  1. "The quick response impressed the client."
  2. "She confidently presented her ideas."
  3. "Wow, that was amazing!"
  4. "The meeting will start after lunch."
  5. "I want coffee and a bagel."
Show Answers
  1. Adjective (describes "response")
  2. Adverb (describes "presented")
  3. Adjective (describes "that")
  4. Preposition (shows time relationship)
  5. Conjunction (connects "coffee" and "bagel")
Pro Tip: Understanding parts of speech helps you:
  • Write clearer sentences
  • Avoid grammar mistakes in emails
  • Use autocorrect more effectively
  • Communicate more professionally
