Lesson 5: Data Analysis

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate and interpret measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
  • Calculate and interpret measures of spread (range, variance, standard deviation)
  • Analyze real-world datasets from sports and social media
  • Identify outliers and understand their impact on statistics

1. Measures of Central Tendency

What is Central Tendency?

Central tendency measures help us find the "center" or "typical value" of a dataset. The three main measures are:

  • Mean: The average of all values
  • Median: The middle value when data is ordered
  • Mode: The most frequently occurring value

Example 1: Basketball Player Stats

Here are the points scored by a basketball player in their last 15 games:

Data: 18, 22, 25, 19, 31, 22, 28, 22, 20, 35, 19, 22, 24, 21, 22

Calculating the Mean:

Mean = Sum of all values ÷ Number of values
Mean = (18 + 22 + 25 + 19 + 31 + 22 + 28 + 22 + 20 + 35 + 19 + 22 + 24 + 21 + 22) ÷ 15
Mean = 350 ÷ 15 = 23.33 points

Finding the Median:

First, order the data: 18, 19, 19, 20, 21, 22, 22, 22, 22, 22, 24, 25, 28, 31, 35

Median = 22 points (the middle value, 8th position)

Finding the Mode:

Mode = 22 points (appears 6 times, more than any other value)

2. Measures of Spread

Why Do We Need Measures of Spread?

Two datasets can have the same mean but very different distributions. Measures of spread tell us how "spread out" the data is from the center.

  • Range: Maximum value - Minimum value
  • Variance: Average of squared differences from the mean
  • Standard Deviation: Square root of variance (in same units as data)

Example 2: Social Media Engagement Comparison

Two influencers post content daily. Here are their likes over 7 days:

Calculating Range:

Influencer A: Range = 520 - 480 = 40 likes

Influencer B: Range = 700 - 200 = 500 likes

Calculating Standard Deviation for Influencer A:

Step 1: Find differences from mean: 0, 20, -10, 10, 5, -5, -20
Step 2: Square each difference: 0, 400, 100, 100, 25, 25, 400
Step 3: Find average (variance): 1050 ÷ 7 = 150
Step 4: Take square root: √150 ≈ 12.25 likes

Interpretation: Influencer A has very consistent engagement (low standard deviation), while Influencer B's posts vary widely in popularity (high standard deviation).

3. Outliers and Their Impact

What Are Outliers?

Outliers are data points that are significantly different from other observations. They can:

  • Dramatically affect the mean
  • Have little effect on the median
  • Increase the standard deviation

When to use median vs mean: Use the median when data has outliers; use the mean for normal distributions.

Example 3: Gaming Scores

A gamer's scores over 10 matches:

Regular games: 1200, 1350, 1280, 1420, 1310, 1390, 1260, 1330, 1370

With one lucky game: 1200, 1350, 1280, 1420, 1310, 1390, 1260, 1330, 1370, 4500

Measure Without Outlier With Outlier (4500) Change
Mean 1323.33 1641 +317.67 (24%)
Median 1330 1340 +10 (0.75%)
Mode None None No change

Conclusion: The median (1340) better represents the typical performance than the mean (1641).

Practice Problems

1. A soccer team's goals per game over 12 matches: 2, 1, 3, 2, 0, 4, 2, 1, 2, 5, 2, 1

  • Calculate the mean, median, and mode
  • Calculate the range and standard deviation
  • Which measure best represents typical performance? Why?

2. YouTube video views for two creators over a week:

  • Creator X: 5000, 5200, 4900, 5100, 5300, 5000, 4800
  • Creator Y: 2000, 8000, 3000, 7500, 2500, 9000, 1000

Compare their mean views and standard deviations. What does this tell you about consistency?

3. A student's test scores: 78, 82, 85, 79, 92, 81, 88, 35 (was sick)

  • Calculate mean with and without the outlier
  • Which measure (mean or median) should the teacher use for grading? Explain.

4. Survey data shows daily screen time (minutes): 120, 180, 150, 200, 165, 190, 175, 145, 210, 160

  • Calculate all measures of central tendency
  • Calculate variance and standard deviation
  • Identify any potential outliers

Key Takeaways

  • Mean is sensitive to outliers; median is resistant to outliers
  • Standard deviation measures consistency - low SD means consistent data
  • Always look at both central tendency AND spread to understand data fully
  • Real-world context matters when interpreting statistics
  • Choose the right measure for your purpose: mean for normal data, median for skewed data
