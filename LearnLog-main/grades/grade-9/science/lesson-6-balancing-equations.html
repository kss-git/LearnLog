Unit 2: Chemical Reactions

Lesson 6: Balancing Chemical Equations

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the Law of Conservation of Mass
  • Learn systematic methods for balancing chemical equations
  • Apply balancing skills to real-world reactions

Why Balance Chemical Equations?

Law of Conservation of Mass

Matter cannot be created or destroyed in a chemical reaction. The number of atoms of each element must be the same on both sides of the equation.

Reading Chemical Equations

Example: Burning Methane (Natural Gas)

CH₄ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O (unbalanced)

Element Reactants Products Balanced?
C (Carbon) 1 1 ✓ Yes
H (Hydrogen) 4 2 ✗ No
O (Oxygen) 2 3 ✗ No

This equation is NOT balanced! We need to add coefficients.

Step-by-Step Balancing Method

Rules for Balancing

  1. Never change subscripts (they define the molecule)
  2. Only add coefficients in front of molecules
  3. Coefficients multiply everything in the molecule
  4. Start with the most complex molecule
  5. Balance one element at a time
  6. Save hydrogen and oxygen for last (they're common)

Balancing Methane Combustion

Step 1: Write the unbalanced equation

CH₄ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O

Step 2: Carbon is already balanced (1 on each side)

CH₄ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O

Step 3: Balance hydrogen - 4 H on left, need 4 on right

CH₄ + O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O

Now we have 4 H atoms on both sides ✓

Step 4: Count oxygen - left has 2, right has 2+2=4

CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O

Now we have 4 O atoms on both sides ✓

Final Balanced Equation:

CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O

Real-World Application: Cooking with Baking Soda

When Baking Soda Meets Acid

Baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) reacts with acids in recipes to make baked goods rise.

Unbalanced: NaHCO₃ + HCl → NaCl + H₂O + CO₂

Let's check:

Element Left Right
Na 1 1
H 1+1=2 2
C 1 1
O 3 1+2=3
Cl 1 1

This equation is already balanced! The CO₂ gas produced creates bubbles that make cakes fluffy.

Challenge: Balancing Combustion in Car Engines

Octane Combustion (Gasoline)

Unbalanced: C₈H₁₈ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O

Step 1: Balance carbon (8 C atoms)

C₈H₁₈ + O₂ → 8CO₂ + H₂O

Step 2: Balance hydrogen (18 H atoms)

C₈H₁₈ + O₂ → 8CO₂ + 9H₂O

Step 3: Count oxygen on right: 8(2) + 9(1) = 25 O atoms

Need 25 O atoms, so 25/2 = 12.5 O₂ molecules

Step 4: Multiply everything by 2 to avoid fractions

2C₈H₁₈ + 25O₂ → 16CO₂ + 18H₂O

Why this matters: This equation shows that burning 2 gallons of gasoline requires 25 molecules of oxygen and produces 16 molecules of CO₂ - which is why car exhaust contributes to greenhouse gases!

Key Takeaways

  • Law of Conservation of Mass: atoms cannot be created or destroyed
  • Balance equations by adding coefficients, never changing subscripts
  • Coefficients multiply the entire molecule
  • Check your work by counting atoms of each element
  • Balanced equations show the exact ratio of reactants needed
  • Real applications: cooking (baking soda), combustion engines, battery chemistry
