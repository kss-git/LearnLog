Understanding Constant Growth and Change

What are Linear Functions?

Linear functions create straight-line graphs and represent situations where something changes at a constant rate. They follow the pattern:

y = mx + b

m = slope (rate of change)
b = y-intercept (starting value)

Where you see linear functions in tech:

  • Subscription pricing (monthly fees)
  • Data usage charges
  • Social media follower growth
  • Gaming progression systems

Slope (m): The Rate of Change

Slope tells you how steep the line is and whether it goes up or down.

Twitch Follower Growth Example

A streamer gains 25 followers per stream:

f(x) = 25x + 100

where x = number of streams, starting with 100 followers

  • Slope (m) = 25 followers per stream
  • Y-intercept (b) = 100 initial followers

Slope Formula

m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)

From (2, 150) to (4, 200):
m = (200 - 150) / (4 - 2) = 50/2 = 25 followers per stream

Y-Intercept (b): The Starting Point

The y-intercept is where the line crosses the y-axis (when x = 0). It represents your starting value.

Subscription Service Example: Spotify Premium

Cost(x) = 10x + 0

where x = months subscribed

  • $10/month = slope (constant rate)
  • $0 = y-intercept (no signup fee)

After 6 months: Cost(6) = 10(6) + 0 = $60

Adobe Creative Cloud Example

Cost(x) = 20x + 50

where x = months subscribed

  • $20/month = slope (monthly fee)
  • $50 = y-intercept (one-time setup fee)

After 6 months: Cost(6) = 20(6) + 50 = $170

Positive vs Negative Slopes

Positive Slope Examples

  • Social media followers increasing
  • Gaming XP accumulation
  • Subscription costs over time
  • Video views growing

Negative Slope Examples

  • Phone battery draining
  • Download progress remaining
  • Gaming health decreasing
  • Free trial days left

Real-World Application: Mobile Data Plan

Your phone plan charges $30/month plus $10 per extra GB of data used beyond your limit.

Cost(g) = 10g + 30

where g = extra GB used

Extra GB (g) Calculation Total Cost
0 10(0) + 30 $30
2 10(2) + 30 $50
5 10(5) + 30 $80
10 10(10) + 30 $130

Practice Problems

Problem 1: YouTube Channel Growth

A YouTuber starts with 500 subscribers and gains 40 new subscribers per video uploaded. Write a linear function and find subscribers after 15 videos.

Show Solution

S(v) = 40v + 500

where v = videos uploaded

S(15) = 40(15) + 500 = 600 + 500 = 1,100 subscribers

Problem 2: Gaming Tournament Scoring

In Fortnite Arena, you start with 0 points and earn 20 points per match won. After 8 wins, you have 160 points. What's the slope and write the function.

Show Solution

Slope = 20 points per win

P(w) = 20w + 0 or simply P(w) = 20w

where w = wins

Check: P(8) = 20(8) = 160 ✓

Problem 3: E-commerce Shipping Costs

An online store charges $5 base shipping plus $2 per item. Write the function and find the cost for ordering 7 items.

Show Solution

C(n) = 2n + 5

where n = number of items

C(7) = 2(7) + 5 = 14 + 5 = $19 shipping

Problem 4: Phone Battery Drain

Your phone battery is at 100% and drains 8% per hour of gaming. Write a linear function (hint: negative slope!) and find battery percentage after 5 hours.

Show Solution

B(h) = -8h + 100

where h = hours of gaming

B(5) = -8(5) + 100 = -40 + 100 = 60% battery remaining

Note: The negative slope (-8) represents decrease

Key Takeaways

  • Linear functions have the form y = mx + b
  • Slope (m) = rate of change (how fast something increases/decreases)
  • Y-intercept (b) = starting value (when x = 0)
  • Positive slope = going up, Negative slope = going down
  • Linear functions model constant growth/decay in real apps and services
