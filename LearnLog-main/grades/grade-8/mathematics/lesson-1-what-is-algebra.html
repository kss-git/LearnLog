📚 What You'll Learn

Algebra is like a secret code that mathematicians use to solve problems. Instead of working with just numbers, we use letters (called variables) to represent unknown or changing values.

Think of it this way: If regular math is like following a recipe with exact measurements, algebra is like creating your own recipe where you can adjust ingredients based on how many people you're serving!

🌟 Why This Matters

Algebra is everywhere in your daily life:

  • Gaming: Calculating damage formulas, XP needed for next level, optimal build stats
  • Shopping: Finding the best deal when prices vary, calculating discounts
  • Social Media: Understanding growth rates, engagement formulas
  • Sports: Predicting scores, analyzing player statistics
  • Technology: Every app, game, and website runs on algebraic equations!

Without algebra, we wouldn't have smartphones, streaming services, or even GPS navigation!

💡 Core Concept: Understanding Variables

What is a Variable?

A variable is a letter (like x, y, n, or any letter really) that represents a number we don't know yet or that can change.

Example: Let's say you're saving money for a new game.
  • m = money you have saved
  • g = cost of the game ($60)
  • n = number of weeks you've been saving

What is an Expression?

An algebraic expression combines numbers, variables, and operations (+, −, ×, ÷) to represent something.

Example: If you save $10 every week, after n weeks you'll have:

10n

This means: 10 × n (10 dollars times the number of weeks)

🎨 Visual: Variables in Action

The formula 10n tells us exactly how much money we'll have after any number of weeks!

✏️ Example 1: Writing Expressions

Problem: You're watching your favorite streamer who has 500 subscribers. They gain 25 new subscribers every day. Write an expression for their total subscribers after d days.

Solution:

  1. Identify what changes: The number of days (d)
  2. Identify what stays the same:
    • Starting subscribers: 500
    • Daily gain: 25
  3. Build the expression:
    • New subscribers after d days = 25 × d = 25d
    • Total = Starting + New = 500 + 25d

Answer: 500 + 25d

💡 Why this works: The expression means "start with 500, then add 25 for each day that passes." If d = 0 (today), total = 500. If d = 10, total = 500 + 250 = 750 subscribers!

✏️ Example 2: Evaluating Expressions

Problem: In a battle royale game, you earn points using the formula: P = 50k + 20, where k is the number of eliminations. If you got 7 eliminations, how many points did you earn?

Solution:

  1. Write down the formula: P = 50k + 20
  2. Identify the value: k = 7 (eliminations)
  3. Substitute k = 7 into the formula:

    P = 50(7) + 20

    P = 350 + 20

    P = 370

Answer: 370 points 🎉

💡 Why this works: We replaced the variable k with 7, then followed the order of operations (multiply first, then add). The +20 is like a "bonus" you always get just for playing!

🎮 Quick Check: Test Your Understanding!

Question: If the expression for your phone's battery life is 100 - 5h (where h is hours of use), how much battery will you have after 3 hours?

Answer: 85%

Solution:

100 - 5h

= 100 - 5(3)

= 100 - 15

= 85%

You started with 100%, and lost 5% per hour for 3 hours!

📝 Practice Questions

Question 1 (Easy): Which of these is a variable?

A) 5
B) x
C) +
D) 12

Answer: B) x

A variable is a letter that represents a number. The others are: 5 and 12 are constants (fixed numbers), and + is an operation.

Question 2 (Medium): A pizza costs $15, and each topping costs $2 extra. Write an expression for the total cost if you order t toppings.

Answer: 15 + 2t

Explanation: You start with the base cost of $15, then add $2 for each topping. If t = 3 toppings, the cost is 15 + 2(3) = 15 + 6 = $21.

Question 3 (Medium): Evaluate the expression 3n - 7 when n = 5.

Answer: 8

Step-by-step:

3n - 7

= 3(5) - 7

= 15 - 7

= 8

Question 4 (Hard): You have $200 saved. You spend $25 per week on activities and earn $40 per week from a part-time job. Write an expression for how much money you'll have after w weeks.

Answer: 200 + 15w

Explanation:

• You start with $200

• Each week you earn $40 but spend $25, so net gain = $40 - $25 = $15 per week

• After w weeks: 200 + 15w

Alternative form: 200 + 40w - 25w (also correct!)

Question 5 (Hard): In a game, your character's health is given by H = 100 + 10L - 5D, where L is your level and D is damage taken. If you're level 8 and took 12 damage, what's your health?

Answer: 120 HP

Solution:

H = 100 + 10L - 5D

H = 100 + 10(8) - 5(12)

H = 100 + 80 - 60

H = 120

You gained 80 HP from leveling but lost 60 from damage!

📌 Quick Summary

  • Algebra uses letters (variables) to represent unknown or changing numbers
  • A variable is a letter like x, n, or t that stands for a number
  • An expression combines numbers, variables, and operations (like 5x + 3)
  • To evaluate an expression, replace the variable with a number and calculate
  • Algebra helps us solve real-world problems where values change or are unknown

🌍 Real-World Connection: Game Developers

Every video game you play is built on algebraic expressions! Game developers use algebra to:

  • Damage formulas: Calculate how much damage your attack does based on your stats
  • Movement speed: Figure out how fast your character runs based on equipment
  • Progression systems: Determine XP needed for each level
  • Economy balance: Set fair prices for in-game items

When Fortnite developers decide that a shotgun does "base damage × distance multiplier × headshot bonus," that's algebra in action! Without it, games would be impossible to balance or even create.

🚀 Challenge Problem

The Netflix Problem:

You and your friends are splitting a Netflix subscription. The premium plan costs $20/month and allows 4 screens. You decide that each person pays an equal share, plus an extra $2 to save money for the next month.

Part A: Write an expression for how much each person pays if there are p people in the group.

Part B: If 4 friends are sharing (p = 4), how much does each person pay?

Part C: How much money will you have saved after 6 months?

Hint for Part A: Each person's payment has two parts:

1. Their share of the $20 cost (divide $20 by the number of people)

2. The extra $2 for savings

Add these together!

Part A Answer: 20/p + 2 or (20 + 2p)/p

Explanation: Split $20 among p people (20/p), then add $2 per person.

Part B Answer: $7 per person

Solution:

= 20/4 + 2

= 5 + 2

= $7

Part C Answer: $48

Solution: 4 people × $2 savings × 6 months = $48 saved!

With 4 people, you collect $8 in savings each month (4 × $2), so after 6 months you have 6 × $8 = $48. That's almost 2.5 months of free Netflix! 🎉
