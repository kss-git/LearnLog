📚 What You'll Learn

Word problems can feel scary because they're written in sentences, not numbers. But here's the secret: every word problem is just a story that's hiding an equation!

Your job is to be a translator - converting English into math. Once you have the equation, you already know how to solve it!

The 4-Step Method:

1️⃣ Read carefully - What are you trying to find?

2️⃣ Define the variable - Let x = the unknown

3️⃣ Write the equation - Translate words to math

4️⃣ Solve and check - Does the answer make sense?

💡 Core Concept: Translating Words to Math

Common Translation Phrases

Learn to recognize these key phrases:

Addition (+):
  • "sum of" - the sum of x and 5 → x + 5
  • "more than" - 7 more than x → x + 7
  • "increased by" - x increased by 3 → x + 3
  • "total" - total of 10 and x → 10 + x
Subtraction (−):
  • "difference of" - difference of x and 4 → x - 4
  • "less than" - 5 less than x → x - 5
  • "decreased by" - x decreased by 8 → x - 8
  • "minus" - x minus 2 → x - 2
Multiplication (×):
  • "product of" - product of 6 and x → 6x
  • "times" - 3 times x → 3x
  • "of" (with fractions/percents) - 50% of x → 0.5x
  • "twice/double" - twice x → 2x
Division (÷):
  • "quotient of" - quotient of x and 4 → x ÷ 4
  • "divided by" - x divided by 5 → x/5
  • "per" - cost per person → total ÷ people
  • "split equally" - split x among 3 → x/3
Equals (=):
  • "is" - x is 10 → x = 10
  • "equals" - total equals 50 → T = 50
  • "results in" - this results in 20 → = 20

✏️ Example 1: Age Problem

Problem: Sarah is 3 years older than twice her brother's age. If Sarah is 17, how old is her brother?

Solution:

  1. Read: We need to find the brother's age
  2. Define: Let b = brother's age
  3. Translate:
    • "Twice brother's age" = 2b
    • "3 years older than" = 2b + 3
    • "Sarah is 17" = 2b + 3 = 17
  4. Solve:

    2b + 3 = 17

    2b = 17 - 3

    2b = 14

    b = 7

  5. Check: If brother is 7, then Sarah is 2(7) + 3 = 14 + 3 = 17 ✅

Answer: The brother is 7 years old

💡 Key Phrase: "3 years older than" means we ADD 3, but we add it to "twice brother's age" (2b), not just b!

✏️ Example 2: Money Problem

Problem: At a gaming tournament, entry costs $15 and each round costs $3. If you spent $36 total, how many rounds did you play?

Solution:

  1. Read: Find the number of rounds played
  2. Define: Let r = number of rounds
  3. Translate:

    Entry fee + (cost per round × rounds) = total

    15 + 3r = 36

  4. Solve:

    15 + 3r = 36

    3r = 36 - 15

    3r = 21

    r = 7

  5. Check: 15 + 3(7) = 15 + 21 = 36 ✅

Answer: You played 7 rounds 🎮

💡 Pattern Recognition: Fixed cost + (variable cost × quantity) = total. This pattern appears in phone bills, subscriptions, taxi fares, and more!

🎮 Quick Check: Translate This!

Question: "The sum of a number and 12 is equal to 3 times the number decreased by 4." Write the equation and solve.

Equation: x + 12 = 3x - 4

Translation:

• "Sum of a number and 12" = x + 12

• "3 times the number" = 3x

• "decreased by 4" = 3x - 4

• "is equal to" = =

Solution:

x + 12 = 3x - 4

12 + 4 = 3x - x

16 = 2x

x = 8 ✓

📝 Practice Questions

Question 1 (Easy): A number increased by 9 equals 25. Find the number.

Answer: 16

Let x = the number

x + 9 = 25

x = 25 - 9

x = 16

Question 2 (Medium): You buy 5 identical notebooks and get $7 change from a $20 bill. How much did each notebook cost?

Answer: $2.60 per notebook

Let n = cost per notebook

5n + 7 = 20 (cost + change = total paid)

5n = 13

n = 2.6 = $2.60

Check: 5(2.60) + 7 = 13 + 7 = 20 ✅

Question 3 (Medium): Your Spotify playlist has 45 songs. This is 3 fewer than twice the number of songs on your friend's playlist. How many songs does your friend have?

Answer: 24 songs

Let f = friend's songs

"3 fewer than twice friend's songs" = 2f - 3

2f - 3 = 45

2f = 48

f = 24

Check: 2(24) - 3 = 48 - 3 = 45 ✅

Question 4 (Hard): Two friends share the cost of a $48 gift. One friend pays $6 more than the other. How much did each friend pay?

Answer: $21 and $27

Let x = amount the first friend paid

Then x + 6 = amount second friend paid

Together: x + (x + 6) = 48

2x + 6 = 48

2x = 42

x = 21

First friend: $21, Second friend: $27

Check: 21 + 27 = 48 ✅ and 27 - 21 = 6 ✅

Question 5 (Hard): In a mobile game, you earn 20 coins per level plus a 50-coin daily bonus. After playing for some number of levels today, you have 370 coins. How many levels did you complete?

Answer: 16 levels

Let L = number of levels

Coins = 20L + 50 (20 per level + 50 bonus)

20L + 50 = 370

20L = 320

L = 16 levels

Check: 20(16) + 50 = 320 + 50 = 370 ✅

📌 Quick Summary

  • Word problems hide equations inside sentences - your job is to translate
  • Always define your variable clearly: "Let x = ..."
  • Look for key phrases: "more than" (+), "less than" (-), "times" (×), "per" (÷)
  • Use the 4-step method: Read, Define, Translate, Solve, Check
  • Always check if your answer makes sense in the real-world context

🌍 Real-World Connection: How Businesses Use Word Problems

Every business decision involves word problems. Here's a real example from a coffee shop:

Business Problem:

"We have $2,000 to spend on supplies. Coffee beans cost $12 per pound, and we need 50 pounds. The rest goes to milk at $4 per gallon. How many gallons of milk can we buy?"

Solution:

Let g = gallons of milk

Coffee cost + Milk cost = Total budget

12(50) + 4g = 2000

600 + 4g = 2000

4g = 1400

g = 350 gallons

Store managers, event planners, project managers - they all solve word problems like this daily to make smart decisions!

🚀 Challenge Problem: The Phone Plan Dilemma

You're choosing between two phone plans:

  • Plan A: $30/month + $0.15 per text
  • Plan B: $50/month with unlimited texts

Part A: Write expressions for the monthly cost of each plan if you send t texts.

Part B: For what number of texts would both plans cost the same?

Part C: If you typically send 200 texts per month, which plan should you choose and how much will you save per year?

Hint for Part A:

Plan A: base cost + (cost per text × number of texts)

Plan B: just the base cost (unlimited texts)

Hint for Part B: Set the two expressions equal to each other

Part A Answer:

Plan A cost: 30 + 0.15t

Plan B cost: 50

Part B Answer: 133.33 texts (about 133 or 134)

Solution:

30 + 0.15t = 50

0.15t = 20

t = 20 ÷ 0.15

t ≈ 133.33 texts

At exactly 133-134 texts, both plans cost about the same!

Part C Answer: Choose Plan A, save $180/year

Calculation:

Plan A with 200 texts: 30 + 0.15(200) = 30 + 30 = $60/month

Plan B: $50/month

Wait... Plan B is cheaper! $50 vs $60 per month

Monthly savings: $60 - $50 = $10

Yearly savings: 12 × $10 = $120/year with Plan B! 💰

Key insight: Since 200 > 133, Plan B becomes the better deal!
