What is a Derivative?

A derivative measures how fast something is changing. It's the instantaneous rate of change - the slope of a curve at a single point.

Definition: f'(x) = limh→0 [f(x+h) - f(x)]/h

The derivative tells us the slope of the tangent line at any point on a curve.

📊 Visualizing the Derivative

The derivative at a point is the slope of the tangent line at that point.

The derivative is the slope of the red tangent line. As we make h smaller, the secant line (blue) approaches the tangent line.

🚗 Real-World: Velocity and Acceleration

A car's position is s(t) = 2t³ - 5t² + 3t meters at time t seconds.

Velocity: v(t) = s'(t) = 6t² - 10t + 3

Acceleration: a(t) = v'(t) = s''(t) = 12t - 10

At t = 2 seconds:

  • Velocity: v(2) = 6(4) - 10(2) + 3 = 24 - 20 + 3 = 7 m/s
  • Acceleration: a(2) = 12(2) - 10 = 24 - 10 = 14 m/s²

📐 Power Rule - The Shortcut!

Instead of using limits every time, we have rules. The most important is the Power Rule:

If f(x) = xⁿ, then f'(x) = n·xⁿ⁻¹

Example 1: f(x) = x⁵

f'(x) = 5x⁴

Example 2: f(x) = 3x⁴ - 2x³ + 5x - 7

f'(x) = 12x³ - 6x² + 5

Example 3: f(x) = 1/x² = x⁻²

f'(x) = -2x⁻³ = -2/x³

🎯 Practice Problems

Problem 1: Basic Derivative

Find the derivative: f(x) = x⁶ + 3x² - 8

Show Solution

Apply the power rule to each term:

f'(x) = 6x⁵ + 6x - 0 = 6x⁵ + 6x

Problem 2: Velocity Problem

A particle's position is s(t) = t³ - 6t² + 9t. Find when the particle is at rest.

Show Solution

At rest means velocity = 0:

v(t) = s'(t) = 3t² - 12t + 9 = 0

3(t² - 4t + 3) = 0

3(t - 1)(t - 3) = 0

The particle is at rest at t = 1 and t = 3 seconds

Problem 3: Finding Slope

Find the slope of y = 2x³ - 5x at the point (2, 6).

Show Solution

The slope is the derivative evaluated at x = 2:

y' = 6x² - 5

At x = 2: y'(2) = 6(4) - 5 = 24 - 5 = 19

🌟 Challenge Problem

Advanced: A rocket's height is h(t) = 100t - 5t² meters. Find:

  • a) The velocity at t = 5 seconds
  • b) When the rocket reaches maximum height
  • c) The maximum height
Show Solution

a) v(t) = h'(t) = 100 - 10t

v(5) = 100 - 10(5) = 50 m/s

b) Maximum height when v(t) = 0:

100 - 10t = 0 → t = 10 seconds

c) Maximum height:

h(10) = 100(10) - 5(100) = 1000 - 500 = 500 meters

🔑 Key Takeaways

  • The derivative measures instantaneous rate of change
  • Geometrically, it's the slope of the tangent line
  • In motion: velocity is the derivative of position, acceleration is the derivative of velocity
  • Power Rule: d/dx[xⁿ] = n·xⁿ⁻¹ makes calculations easy
