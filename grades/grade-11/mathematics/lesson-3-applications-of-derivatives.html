Optimization and Real-World Problem Solving

Why Applications Matter

Derivatives aren't just abstract math - they solve real problems! Engineers use them to minimize material costs, physicists use them to analyze motion, and economists use them to maximize profit.

Key Idea: At maximum or minimum points, the derivative equals zero (the tangent is horizontal).

📊 Finding Maximum and Minimum Values

Critical points occur where f'(x) = 0 or f'(x) is undefined.

📦 Optimization Example: Maximum Volume

Problem: You have a 12 cm × 12 cm piece of cardboard. Cut equal squares from each corner and fold up the sides to make a box. What size squares maximize the volume?

Solution:

1. Volume: V(x) = x(12-2x)(12-2x) = x(144 - 48x + 4x²) = 4x³ - 48x² + 144x

2. Find derivative: V'(x) = 12x² - 96x + 144

3. Set equal to zero: 12x² - 96x + 144 = 0

→ x² - 8x + 12 = 0

→ (x - 2)(x - 6) = 0

→ x = 2 or x = 6

4. Test: x = 6 makes dimensions negative, so x = 2 cm

5. Maximum volume: V(2) = 2(8)(8) = 128 cm³

💰 Business Application: Maximizing Profit

A company's profit function is P(x) = -2x² + 800x - 5000 dollars, where x is thousands of units produced.

Find maximum profit:

P'(x) = -4x + 800 = 0

x = 200 thousand units

Maximum profit: P(200) = -2(40000) + 800(200) - 5000

= -80000 + 160000 - 5000 = $75,000

🎯 Practice Problems

Problem 1: Finding Extrema

Find the maximum and minimum values of f(x) = x³ - 3x² - 9x + 5 on the interval [-2, 4].

Show Solution

f'(x) = 3x² - 6x - 9 = 3(x² - 2x - 3) = 3(x-3)(x+1) = 0

Critical points: x = -1, x = 3

Check endpoints and critical points:

  • f(-2) = -8 - 12 + 18 + 5 = 3
  • f(-1) = -1 - 3 + 9 + 5 = 10 (local max)
  • f(3) = 27 - 27 - 27 + 5 = -22 (local min)
  • f(4) = 64 - 48 - 36 + 5 = -15

Maximum: 10 at x = -1; Minimum: -22 at x = 3

Problem 2: Fencing Optimization

A farmer has 200 meters of fence to enclose a rectangular field. What dimensions give maximum area?

Show Solution

Let width = x, then length = (200 - 2x)/2 = 100 - x

Area: A(x) = x(100 - x) = 100x - x²

A'(x) = 100 - 2x = 0 → x = 50 m

Length = 100 - 50 = 50 m

Maximum area: 50 × 50 = 2500 m² (a square!)

Problem 3: Related Rates

A balloon is being inflated. Its radius increases at 2 cm/s. How fast is the volume increasing when r = 5 cm? (V = 4/3πr³)

Show Solution

Given: dr/dt = 2 cm/s, find dV/dt when r = 5

V = 4/3πr³

dV/dt = 4πr² · dr/dt

dV/dt = 4π(5)²(2) = 4π(25)(2) = 200π ≈ 628.3 cm³/s

🌟 Challenge Problem

Advanced: A cylindrical can must hold 500 cm³ of liquid. Find the dimensions (radius and height) that minimize the amount of material used (surface area).

Hint: V = πr²h = 500, and Surface Area = 2πr² + 2πrh

Show Solution

From V = 500: h = 500/(πr²)

Surface Area: S = 2πr² + 2πr · 500/(πr²) = 2πr² + 1000/r

S'(r) = 4πr - 1000/r² = 0

4πr³ = 1000

r³ = 250/π

r = ∛(250/π) ≈ 4.3 cm

h = 500/(π·4.3²) ≈ 8.6 cm

Notice: h = 2r (height equals diameter!)

🔑 Key Takeaways

  • At maximum/minimum points, the derivative equals zero (horizontal tangent)
  • Always check critical points AND endpoints for absolute extrema
  • Optimization: write equation, take derivative, solve for zero, verify
  • Real applications: business profit, engineering design, physics problems
