Lesson 8: Common Grammar Errors

Mistakes Everyone Makes (and How to Fix Them)

The Top 20 Grammar Mistakes

1️⃣Your vs. You're
WRONG

"Your going to love this!"

"Is that you're phone?"

RIGHT

You're going to love this! (You are)

Is that your phone? (possession)

Memory Trick:

You're = You are (apostrophe replaces 'a')

Your = ownership (like "my," "his," "her")

2️⃣Its vs. It's
Social Media Fail:

"The app lost it's data"

"Its going to rain today"

Correct:

The app lost its data (possession)

It's going to rain today (It is)

Memory Trick:

It's = It is OR It has

Its = belonging to it (no apostrophe, like "his" or "hers")

3️⃣Their, There, They're
Word Meaning Example
Their Possession (belongs to them) Their presentation was excellent.
There Location OR "there is/are" Put it over there. There are two options.
They're They are They're coming to the meeting.
Text Message Fails:

"Their going to be late"

"The files are over they're"

"There presentation needs work"

Fixed:

They're going to be late (They are)

The files are over there (location)

Their presentation needs work (belongs to them)

4️⃣Then vs. Than
THEN = Time/Sequence

First save the file, then close the app.

We had lunch, then went to the meeting.

THAN = Comparison

This laptop is better than that one.

I'd rather email than call.

Email Mistake:

"This option is cheaper then the other one"

Correct:

This option is cheaper than the other one

5️⃣Affect vs. Effect
AFFECT = Verb (to influence)

The bug affects all users.

How will this affect the timeline?

EFFECT = Noun (result)

The update had a positive effect.

What effect will this have?

Memory Trick:

Affect = Action (verb)

Effect = End result (noun)

6️⃣Could of / Should of / Would of
Common Mistakes:

"I could of finished it yesterday"

"You should of told me earlier"

"We would of won if we tried harder"

Correct (Always "HAVE"):

I could have finished it yesterday (or "could've")

You should have told me earlier (or "should've")

We would have won if we tried harder (or "would've")

Why this happens: "Could've" sounds like "could of" when spoken, but it's actually a contraction of "could have"!
7️⃣Me vs. I
Wrong:

"Me and Sarah will handle the project"

"Between you and I, this won't work"

Correct:

Sarah and I will handle the project

Between you and me, this won't work

The Test:

Remove the other person and see if it still makes sense:

  • "Me will handle" ✗ → "I will handle" ✓
  • "Between I" ✗ → "Between me" ✓
8️⃣Who vs. Whom
WHO = Subject (does the action)

Who sent this email?

Who is responsible?

WHOM = Object (receives action)

To whom should I send this?

Whom did you contact?

Easy Test:

Replace with he/him:

  • He sent it → use WHO
  • Send it to him → use WHOM
9️⃣Apostrophes with Plurals
Social Media Fails:

"The 1990's were great"

"I have two phone's"

"All manager's must attend"

Correct:

The 1990s were great (plural, not possessive)

I have two phones (plural)

All managers must attend (plural)

Rule: Use apostrophes for possession, NOT for plurals!
  • Plural: phones, managers, 1990s
  • Possessive: phone's screen, manager's office
🔟Loose vs. Lose
LOOSE = Not tight

The cable is loose.

This shirt fits loose.

LOSE = Misplace or fail

Don't lose this file!

We might lose the game.

Professional Email Killers

Subject-Verb Agreement
❌ Unprofessional Email:

"The team are working on the project. Each of them have their own tasks. The data show interesting results."

✅ Professional Email:

"The team is working on the project. Each of them has their own tasks. The data shows interesting results."

Tricky Words:
  • Team, group, company = singular (use "is," "has")
  • Everyone, each, somebody = singular
  • Data = can be singular or plural (modern usage: singular)
Dangling Modifiers
Confusing:

"After reviewing the report, the errors were obvious."

(The errors reviewed the report?)

Clear:

"After reviewing the report, I found the errors were obvious."

"After I reviewed the report, the errors were obvious."

Double Negatives
Wrong:

"I don't have no time"

"We can't get nothing done"

Correct:

I don't have any time

I have no time

We can't get anything done

Final Practice: Find and Fix the Errors

Each sentence has 1-2 errors. Can you spot them?

  1. "Me and my team could of finished it sooner then expected."
  2. "Your going to loose you're access if you don't update it."
  3. "Their are alot of issue's with the new software."
  4. "The data show that its effecting all user's."
  5. "Between you and I, this should of been done differently."
Show Corrected Versions
  1. "My team and I could have finished it sooner than expected."
  2. "You're going to lose your access if you don't update it."
  3. "There are a lot of issues with the new software."
  4. "The data shows that it's affecting all users."
  5. "Between you and me, this should have been done differently."
Pro Tips to Avoid Errors:
  • 📱 Reread before sending - Even quick texts deserve a glance
  • ✍️ Use autocorrect wisely - But don't trust it blindly (it often confuses their/there/they're!)
  • 📚 Read more - Seeing correct grammar helps you internalize it
  • 🔍 When in doubt, Google it - No shame in checking!
  • 💼 Professional emails matter - Take extra time with work communications

Remember:

Grammar mistakes in casual texts with friends? Totally fine.

Grammar mistakes in job applications, professional emails, or school papers? Can cost you opportunities.

Know your audience, and choose your words carefully!
