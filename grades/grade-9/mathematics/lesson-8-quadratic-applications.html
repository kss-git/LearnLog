Application 1: Projectile Motion

⚽ Problem: Soccer Goal Kick

A soccer player kicks a ball. Its height (in meters) after t seconds is:

h(t) = -5t² + 20t + 1

Questions:

  1. What is the maximum height of the ball?
  2. When does the ball reach maximum height?
  3. When does the ball hit the ground?
  4. What is the initial height (at t = 0)?

Solution

Question 1 & 2: Maximum Height (Vertex)

Find time at maximum: t = -b/2a = -20/2(-5) = -20/-10 = 2 seconds

Find maximum height: h(2) = -5(2)² + 20(2) + 1

= -5(4) + 40 + 1 = -20 + 40 + 1 = 21 meters

Answer: Maximum height is 21 meters at t = 2 seconds

Question 3: When Ball Hits Ground (h = 0)

-5t² + 20t + 1 = 0

Using quadratic formula: a = -5, b = 20, c = 1

t = (-20 ± √(400 - 4(-5)(1))) / 2(-5)

t = (-20 ± √420) / -10

t = (-20 ± 20.49) / -10

t = 0.49/-10 = -0.049 (reject, negative time)

t = -40.49/-10 = 4.05 seconds

Answer: Ball lands at approximately 4.05 seconds

Question 4: Initial Height

h(0) = -5(0)² + 20(0) + 1 = 1 meter

Answer: Ball was kicked from 1 meter high

Application 2: Area Optimization

📐 Problem: Garden Fence

You have 60 meters of fencing to enclose a rectangular garden against a wall. You only need to fence three sides (the wall serves as the fourth side).

Question: What dimensions maximize the garden area?

Solution

Step 1: Set Up Variables

Let x = width of garden (perpendicular to wall)

Let y = length of garden (parallel to wall)

Fencing constraint: 2x + y = 60

Therefore: y = 60 - 2x

Step 2: Create Area Function

Area = length × width = x × y

A(x) = x(60 - 2x)

A(x) = 60x - 2x²

A(x) = -2x² + 60x

Step 3: Find Maximum (Vertex)

This is a parabola opening downward (a = -2 < 0)

x = -b/2a = -60/2(-2) = -60/-4 = 15 meters

y = 60 - 2(15) = 60 - 30 = 30 meters

Maximum area = 15 × 30 = 450 m²

Answer

Width: 15 meters

Length: 30 meters

Maximum Area: 450 square meters

Area Function: A(x) = -2x² + 60x

Application 3: Business - Profit Maximization

💰 Problem: T-Shirt Sales

A company sells t-shirts. Their profit equation is:

P(x) = -2x² + 80x - 200

where x is the selling price in dollars and P is daily profit in dollars.

Questions:

  1. What price maximizes profit?
  2. What is the maximum daily profit?
  3. At what prices does the company break even (P = 0)?

Solution

Question 1 & 2: Maximum Profit

x = -b/2a = -80/2(-2) = -80/-4 = $20

P(20) = -2(20)² + 80(20) - 200

= -2(400) + 1600 - 200 = -800 + 1600 - 200 = $600

Answer: Price at $20 gives maximum profit of $600

Question 3: Break-Even Points

-2x² + 80x - 200 = 0

Divide by -2: x² - 40x + 100 = 0

Using quadratic formula:

x = (40 ± √(1600 - 400)) / 2

x = (40 ± √1200) / 2

x = (40 ± 34.64) / 2

x = 37.32 or x = 2.68

Answer: Break even at $2.68 or $37.32

💡 Business Insight

Prices between $2.68 and $37.32 generate profit. Below $2.68, you're selling too cheap. Above $37.32, the high price drives away customers. The sweet spot is $20!

Application 4: Gaming - Jump Physics

🎮 Problem: Video Game Character Jump

In a platformer game, a character's jump height is modeled by:

h(t) = -16t² + 32t

where h is height in pixels and t is time in seconds.

Questions:

  1. How high does the character jump?
  2. How long is the character in the air?
  3. At what time(s) is the character at 12 pixels high?

Solution

Question 1: Maximum Jump Height

t = -32/2(-16) = -32/-32 = 1 second

h(1) = -16(1)² + 32(1) = -16 + 32 = 16 pixels

Answer: Peak jump is 16 pixels at 1 second

Question 2: Air Time

-16t² + 32t = 0

-16t(t - 2) = 0

t = 0 (start) or t = 2 (land)

Answer: Character is in air for 2 seconds

Question 3: When at 12 Pixels

-16t² + 32t = 12

-16t² + 32t - 12 = 0

Divide by -4: 4t² - 8t + 3 = 0

Factor: (2t - 1)(2t - 3) = 0

t = 0.5 seconds (going up) or t = 1.5 seconds (coming down)

Answer: At 12 pixels at 0.5s and 1.5s

Practice Challenges

EASY

Problem 1: Water Fountain

A water fountain's stream follows h(d) = -d² + 4d, where d is horizontal distance in feet and h is height in feet.

a) What is the maximum height of the water?

b) How far from the fountain does the water land?

a) Maximum height:

d = -4/2(-1) = 2 feet

h(2) = -(2)² + 4(2) = -4 + 8 = 4 feet

Answer: 4 feet high

b) Landing distance:

-d² + 4d = 0

-d(d - 4) = 0

d = 0 or d = 4

Answer: Lands 4 feet away

MEDIUM

Problem 2: Parking Lot Design

A rectangular parking lot is to be built with 200 meters of fencing on three sides (one side is against a building). Find the dimensions that maximize the area.

Let x = width, y = length

Constraint: 2x + y = 200, so y = 200 - 2x

Area: A(x) = x(200 - 2x) = -2x² + 200x

Maximum: x = -200/2(-2) = 50 meters

y = 200 - 2(50) = 100 meters

Answer: 50m × 100m = 5000 m²

MEDIUM

Problem 3: Basketball Shot

A basketball player shoots from 15 feet away. The ball's height is h(x) = -0.06x² + 1.2x + 6, where x is horizontal distance.

a) Does the ball clear a 10-foot high defender at x = 5 feet?

b) What's the ball's maximum height?

a) Height at x = 5:

h(5) = -0.06(25) + 1.2(5) + 6 = -1.5 + 6 + 6 = 10.5 feet

Yes, clears 10-foot defender!

b) Maximum height:

x = -1.2/2(-0.06) = -1.2/-0.12 = 10 feet

h(10) = -0.06(100) + 1.2(10) + 6 = -6 + 12 + 6 = 12 feet

Answer: Maximum is 12 feet

HARD

Problem 4: Concert Revenue

A concert venue holds 500 people. At $30 per ticket, it sells out. For each $2 increase in price, 20 fewer tickets are sold. Find the ticket price that maximizes revenue.

Let x = number of $2 increases

Price: 30 + 2x

Tickets sold: 500 - 20x

Revenue: R(x) = (30 + 2x)(500 - 20x)

= 15000 - 600x + 1000x - 40x²

= -40x² + 400x + 15000

Maximum: x = -400/2(-40) = -400/-80 = 5

Optimal price: 30 + 2(5) = $40

Tickets sold: 500 - 20(5) = 400

Max revenue: 40 × 400 = $16,000

Answer: $40 ticket price maximizes revenue at $16,000

Key Problem-Solving Steps

  1. Read carefully - Identify what you're solving for
  2. Define variables - Let x represent something meaningful
  3. Set up equation - Write the quadratic relationship
  4. Identify what you need - Maximum? Root? Specific value?
  5. Use the right tool - Vertex for max/min, formula for roots
  6. Check your answer - Does it make sense in context?
  7. Answer the question - Include units and full explanation

🎯 Success Tips

  • Draw diagrams to visualize the problem
  • Check if your answer is physically reasonable (no negative time, etc.)
  • Maximum/minimum almost always means find the vertex
  • When it hits the ground/breaks even means set equation to zero
  • Always include units in your final answer
