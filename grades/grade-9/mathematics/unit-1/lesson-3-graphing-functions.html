Why Graph Functions?

Every app you use displays data visually. Graphs make patterns obvious that would be hidden in numbers alone:

  • Spotify shows your listening history as a graph
  • Instagram analytics display follower growth over time
  • Fitness apps chart your workout progress
  • Games show your skill rating trending up or down

The Coordinate Plane

Graphs use two axes to plot points: x (horizontal) and y (vertical).

Graphing Linear Functions

Remember y = mx + b? Let's graph it step by step.

Example: Netflix Viewing Time

You watch 2 hours of Netflix per day, starting from 0:

h(d) = 2d + 0

where d = days, h = total hours watched

Step 1: Find the y-intercept (b = 0)

Start at origin (0, 0)

Step 2: Use slope (m = 2)

For every 1 day right, go up 2 hours

Step 3: Plot points and connect

  • (0, 0) - Day 0: 0 hours
  • (1, 2) - Day 1: 2 hours
  • (2, 4) - Day 2: 4 hours
  • (3, 6) - Day 3: 6 hours

Practice Problems

Problem 1: Plot This Function

Graph y = 3x - 1 by plotting at least 3 points.

Show Solution

Points to plot:

  • x = 0: y = 3(0) - 1 = -1 → (0, -1)
  • x = 1: y = 3(1) - 1 = 2 → (1, 2)
  • x = 2: y = 3(2) - 1 = 5 → (2, 5)

Connect with a straight line going up steeply (slope = 3)

Problem 2: TikTok Followers

Your TikTok has 100 followers and gains 50 per day. Write the function and identify 3 points on the graph.

Show Solution

F(d) = 50d + 100

Points:

  • (0, 100) - Start with 100 followers
  • (1, 150) - After 1 day
  • (5, 350) - After 5 days

Problem 3: Interpret the Graph

A graph shows phone battery % over time. It starts at (0, 100) and ends at (5, 0). What does this mean? What's the slope?

Show Solution

Meaning: Phone started at 100% battery and died (0%) after 5 hours

Slope: m = (0 - 100)/(5 - 0) = -100/5 = -20% per hour

Battery drains 20% every hour

Key Takeaways

  • Graphs visualize functions using x and y coordinates
  • Plot points by finding (x, y) pairs from the function
  • Linear functions create straight lines
  • Slope determines steepness: steep = big changes, flat = small changes
  • Every app uses graphs to show data trends (followers, stats, progress)
