Equations with Variables on Both Sides

Grade 8 | Mathematics | Unit 2: Equations and Inequalities

🏠 Home 📚 Grade 8 📐 Mathematics

⚔️ You and your friend are both grinding XP in the same game. You start with 500 XP and earn 30 per hour. Your friend has 200 XP but earns 50 per hour. When will you both have the same XP? This equation has variables on BOTH sides, and it's surprisingly common in real life!

🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Solve equations with variables on both sides of the equal sign
  • Move variable terms to one side using inverse operations
  • Identify equations with one solution, no solution, or infinite solutions
  • Apply these techniques to comparison problems
  • Verify solutions by substitution

📚 What You'll Learn

When equations have variables on both sides, we're comparing two changing quantities. The solution tells us when those quantities are equal.

Think of it like a race: both runners are moving, and we want to know when (or if) one catches up to the other!

Example:

3x + 5 = 2x + 9

Both sides have variables AND numbers. We need to get all x terms on one side and all numbers on the other!

🌟 Why This Matters

Variables on both sides model comparison situations:

  • Comparing Subscriptions: Which streaming service is cheaper over time?
  • Gaming Progress: When will two players reach the same level?
  • Phone Plans: At what usage amount do two plans cost the same?
  • Savings Accounts: When will two people have equal savings?
  • Business: When will revenue equal costs (break-even point)?

Financial advisors, business analysts, and competitive gamers use these equations to make strategic decisions!

💡 Core Concept: Solving Strategy

The Four-Step Method:

1. Simplify Both Sides

• Distribute and combine like terms on each side

2. Move All Variables to One Side

• Choose the side with more variables (easier!)

• Add or subtract to eliminate variables from one side

3. Move All Numbers to the Other Side

• Use inverse operations

4. Solve for the Variable

• Divide or multiply to isolate the variable

Pro Tip: Choose Wisely!

When deciding which side to collect variables on, pick the side that will give you a positive coefficient. This makes the math easier!

Example: For 2x + 5 = 5x - 4, move to the 5x side (larger coefficient) → less negative numbers!

🎨 Visual: The Balancing Act

Notice how we moved all x terms to the left, then all numbers to the right!

✏️ Example 1: Basic Variable on Both Sides

Problem: Solve: 7x - 4 = 3x + 12

Solution:

  1. Step 1: Move variables to one side (subtract 3x from both)

    7x - 4 = 3x + 12

    7x - 3x - 4 = 3x - 3x + 12

    4x - 4 = 12

  2. Step 2: Move numbers to other side (add 4 to both)

    4x - 4 + 4 = 12 + 4

    4x = 16

  3. Step 3: Isolate x (divide both sides by 4)

    4x ÷ 4 = 16 ÷ 4

    x = 4

  4. Check: 7(4) - 4 = 28 - 4 = 24 AND 3(4) + 12 = 12 + 12 = 24 ✅

Answer: x = 4

💡 Strategy note: We subtracted 3x (smaller coefficient) from both sides to keep positive numbers. Always choose the path that keeps things positive when possible!

✏️ Example 2: Real-World Phone Plan Comparison

Problem: Plan A costs $30/month plus $0.10 per text. Plan B costs $20/month plus $0.15 per text. At how many texts do the plans cost the same?

Equations:

Plan A: 30 + 0.10t

Plan B: 20 + 0.15t

Set equal: 30 + 0.10t = 20 + 0.15t

Solution:

  1. Step 1: Subtract 0.10t from both sides

    30 + 0.10t = 20 + 0.15t

    30 = 20 + 0.05t

  2. Step 2: Subtract 20 from both sides

    30 - 20 = 0.05t

    10 = 0.05t

  3. Step 3: Divide both sides by 0.05

    10 ÷ 0.05 = t

    t = 200

  4. Check:

    Plan A: 30 + 0.10(200) = 30 + 20 = $50

    Plan B: 20 + 0.15(200) = 20 + 30 = $50 ✅

Answer: 200 texts

💡 Real-world insight: If you send fewer than 200 texts, Plan B is cheaper. If you send more than 200 texts, Plan A is cheaper!

⚠️ Special Cases: No Solution & Infinite Solutions

Case 1: No Solution

This happens when variables cancel out and you get a FALSE statement.

Example: 2x + 5 = 2x + 8

2x - 2x + 5 = 8

5 = 8 ❌ (FALSE!)

This means NO SOLUTION exists - like parallel lines that never meet!

Case 2: Infinite Solutions

This happens when variables cancel out and you get a TRUE statement.

Example: 3x + 4 = 3x + 4

3x - 3x + 4 = 4

4 = 4 ✓ (TRUE!)

This means INFINITE SOLUTIONS - any value of x works! The equations are identical.

Summary:

  • One Solution: x = [specific number]
  • No Solution: FALSE statement (like 5 = 8)
  • Infinite Solutions: TRUE statement (like 4 = 4)

🎮 Quick Check: Race to the Finish!

Question: Sarah has $50 and saves $15/week. Marcus has $110 and saves $5/week. After how many weeks will they have the same amount saved?

Set up: 50 + 15w = 110 + 5w

Answer: 6 weeks

Solution:

50 + 15w = 110 + 5w

15w - 5w = 110 - 50 (move variables left, numbers right)

10w = 60

w = 6 weeks

Check: Sarah: 50 + 15(6) = $140, Marcus: 110 + 5(6) = $140 ✅

📝 Practice Questions

Question 1 (Easy): Solve: 5x + 2 = 3x + 10

Answer: x = 4

Solution:

5x + 2 = 3x + 10

5x - 3x = 10 - 2 (subtract 3x, subtract 2)

2x = 8

x = 4

Question 2 (Medium): Solve: 4x - 7 = 2x + 9

Answer: x = 8

Solution:

4x - 7 = 2x + 9

4x - 2x = 9 + 7

2x = 16

x = 8

Question 3 (Medium): Does this equation have one solution, no solution, or infinite solutions? 3x + 5 = 3x - 2

Answer: No Solution

Explanation:

3x + 5 = 3x - 2

3x - 3x + 5 = -2

5 = -2 ❌ (FALSE statement!)

Since 5 never equals -2, there's no value of x that makes this true.

Question 4 (Hard): Two ride-share services: RideNow charges $5 plus $1.50/mile. QuickRide charges $8 plus $1/mile. At what distance do they cost the same?

Answer: 6 miles

Equation: 5 + 1.50m = 8 + 1m

Solution:

5 + 1.50m = 8 + 1m

1.50m - 1m = 8 - 5

0.50m = 3

m = 6 miles 🚗

Both cost $14 at 6 miles!

Question 5 (Hard): Solve: 2(3x + 1) = 5x - 4

Answer: x = -6

Solution:

2(3x + 1) = 5x - 4

6x + 2 = 5x - 4 (distribute the 2)

6x - 5x = -4 - 2 (move variables and numbers)

x = -6

Check: 2(3(-6) + 1) = 2(-18 + 1) = 2(-17) = -34

5(-6) - 4 = -30 - 4 = -34 ✅

📌 Quick Summary

  • Equations with variables on both sides compare two changing quantities
  • Strategy: Move all variables to one side, all numbers to the other
  • Choose the side with the larger coefficient to keep positive numbers
  • Special cases: No solution (false statement) or infinite solutions (true statement)
  • Always check your answer by substituting back into the original equation

🌍 Real-World Connection: Break-Even Analysis

Businesses use variables on both sides to find their break-even point - when revenue equals costs:

Scenario: A game developer wants to launch a new mobile game.

Costs: $50,000 development + $5 per user (server costs)

Revenue: $15 per user (from ads and purchases)

Equation:

Revenue = Costs

15u = 50,000 + 5u

10u = 50,000

u = 5,000 users

The game needs 5,000 users to break even! After that, every new user means profit. Tech companies use this analysis daily to make strategic decisions about pricing, marketing, and features.

🚀 Challenge Problem: The Streaming Wars

You're deciding between two streaming services for your favorite shows:

StreamFlix:

  • $15/month subscription
  • $3 rental fee for new releases

WatchNow:

  • $9/month subscription
  • $5 rental fee for new releases

Part A: Write an equation to find when both services cost the same per month (where n = number of new release rentals).

Part B: Solve to find the break-even point.

Part C: If you typically rent 5 new releases per month, which service should you choose and how much will you save per year?

Hint for Part A:

StreamFlix total: Base subscription + (rental cost × number of rentals)

WatchNow total: Base subscription + (rental cost × number of rentals)

Set them equal!

Part A Answer: 15 + 3n = 9 + 5n

Explanation:

StreamFlix: 15 + 3n

WatchNow: 9 + 5n

Part B Answer: 3 rentals

Solution:

15 + 3n = 9 + 5n

15 - 9 = 5n - 3n

6 = 2n

n = 3 rentals

Both cost $24 when you rent 3 new releases!

Part C Answer: Choose StreamFlix, save $48/year

Calculation at 5 rentals/month:

StreamFlix: 15 + 3(5) = 15 + 15 = $30/month

WatchNow: 9 + 5(5) = 9 + 25 = $34/month

Monthly savings: 34 - 30 = $4

Annual savings: 4 × 12 = $48 per year! 💰

Decision: Since you rent MORE than 3 releases, StreamFlix is the better deal!
