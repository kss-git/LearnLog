Solving Linear Equations

🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Understand what an equation is and how it differs from an expression
  • Use inverse operations to isolate variables
  • Solve one-step and two-step linear equations
  • Check your solutions to verify they're correct
  • Apply equation-solving skills to real-world problems

📚 What You'll Learn

A linear equation is like a balance scale - both sides must be equal. Our goal is to find the value of the unknown variable (usually x) that makes the equation true.

Think of it like unlocking a phone: you need to do the right steps in the right order to reveal the secret number!

Key Difference:

📊 Expression: 3x + 5 (no equals sign - just a phrase)

⚖️ Equation: 3x + 5 = 14 (has an equals sign - a complete sentence that must be true)

🌟 Why This Matters

Solving equations is one of the most practical math skills you'll ever learn:

  • Shopping: "How many $15 shirts can I buy with $100?"
  • Fitness: "If I burn 300 calories per workout, how many workouts to lose 2 pounds?"
  • Gaming: "How many matches do I need to win to reach Diamond rank?"
  • Saving Money: "How many weeks of saving $20 until I can buy that $250 console?"
  • Cooking: "I need to double a recipe - how much of each ingredient?"

Engineers, programmers, scientists, and even social media influencers use equations daily!

💡 Core Concept: The Balance Method

Rule #1: Keep It Balanced

An equation is like a seesaw - whatever you do to one side, you MUST do to the other side to keep it balanced.

Rule #2: Use Inverse Operations

To undo something, do the opposite:

• Addition ↔️ Subtraction

• Multiplication ↔️ Division

Rule #3: Work Backwards

Think of an equation like putting on clothes. To solve it, take them off in reverse order:

👕 Getting Dressed: Underwear → Shirt → Jacket

🔄 Getting Undressed: Jacket → Shirt → Underwear

Same with equations: reverse the order of operations!

🎨 Visual: The Balance Scale Method

If you subtract 3 from the left side, you MUST subtract 3 from the right side too!

✏️ Example 1: One-Step Equation (Addition)

Problem: Solve: x + 12 = 35

Solution:

  1. Goal: Get x by itself on one side
  2. Current obstacle: The +12 is "attached" to x
  3. Inverse operation: Subtract 12 from both sides

    x + 12 = 35

    x + 12 - 12 = 35 - 12

    x = 23 ✓

  4. Check: Does 23 + 12 = 35? Yes! ✅

Answer: x = 23

💡 Real-world meaning: If you have some money (x) and your friend gives you $12, you now have $35 total. You originally had $23!

✏️ Example 2: Two-Step Equation

Problem: You're buying concert tickets. Each ticket costs $25, plus a $8 service fee. If you spent $83 total, how many tickets did you buy?

Equation: 25x + 8 = 83 (where x = number of tickets)

Solution:

  1. Step 1: Subtract 8 from both sides (undo the addition first)

    25x + 8 = 83

    25x + 8 - 8 = 83 - 8

    25x = 75

  2. Step 2: Divide both sides by 25 (undo the multiplication)

    25x = 75

    25x ÷ 25 = 75 ÷ 25

    x = 3 ✓

  3. Check: Does 25(3) + 8 = 83?

    75 + 8 = 83 ✅ Correct!

Answer: You bought 3 tickets! 🎫🎫🎫

💡 Why this order? We use PEMDAS in reverse: undo addition/subtraction first, then multiplication/division.

🎮 Quick Check: Can You Solve It?

Question: Your phone plan costs $30 per month plus $0.10 per text message. This month your bill was $45. How many texts did you send?

Equation: 30 + 0.10t = 45

Answer: 150 texts

Solution:

30 + 0.10t = 45

0.10t = 45 - 30

0.10t = 15

t = 15 ÷ 0.10

t = 150 texts 📱

📝 Practice Questions

Question 1 (Easy): Solve: x - 7 = 15

Answer: x = 22

x - 7 = 15

x - 7 + 7 = 15 + 7 (add 7 to both sides)

x = 22

Check: 22 - 7 = 15 ✅

Question 2 (Easy): Solve: 4x = 32

Answer: x = 8

4x = 32

4x ÷ 4 = 32 ÷ 4 (divide both sides by 4)

x = 8

Check: 4(8) = 32 ✅

Question 3 (Medium): Solve: 2x + 9 = 25

Answer: x = 8

Step 1: Subtract 9 from both sides

2x + 9 - 9 = 25 - 9

2x = 16

Step 2: Divide both sides by 2

2x ÷ 2 = 16 ÷ 2

x = 8

Check: 2(8) + 9 = 16 + 9 = 25 ✅

Question 4 (Medium): You're saving for a $180 skateboard. You already have $45 and plan to save $15 each week. How many weeks until you can buy it?

Hint: 45 + 15w = 180

Answer: 9 weeks

45 + 15w = 180

15w = 180 - 45

15w = 135

w = 135 ÷ 15

w = 9 weeks 🛹

Question 5 (Hard): A ride-sharing trip costs $3 base fee plus $1.50 per mile. If your trip cost $18, how many miles did you travel?

Answer: 10 miles

Set up equation: 3 + 1.50m = 18

Solve:

1.50m = 18 - 3

1.50m = 15

m = 15 ÷ 1.50

m = 10 miles 🚗

📌 Quick Summary

  • An equation has an equals sign and states that two things are equal
  • To solve equations, use inverse operations to isolate the variable
  • Always do the same operation to both sides to keep it balanced
  • For two-step equations: undo addition/subtraction first, then multiplication/division
  • Always check your answer by substituting it back into the original equation

🌍 Real-World Connection: How Spotify Calculates Payments

Ever wonder how musicians get paid on Spotify? They use equations!

The Formula: Payment = (Streams × $0.004) - Platform Fee

If an artist earned $800 last month and the platform fee was $40, we can solve:

Streams × 0.004 - 40 = 800

Streams × 0.004 = 840

Streams = 840 ÷ 0.004

Streams = 210,000 plays! 🎵

Every business, app, and service uses equations like this to calculate costs, profits, and payments!

🚀 Challenge Problem: The Mystery Package

You ordered some items online. The shipping cost is calculated as: $5 base fee + $2 per pound.

Your receipt shows:

  • Item cost: $47
  • Shipping: ?
  • Total: $64

Part A: How much was the shipping cost?

Part B: How many pounds did your package weigh?

Part C: If the shipping was calculated wrong and should have been $2.50 per pound, what should the total have been?

Hint for Part A: Total = Item cost + Shipping

64 = 47 + Shipping

Hint for Part B: Shipping = 5 + 2w (where w = weight)

Part A Answer: $17

Total = Item cost + Shipping

64 = 47 + Shipping

Shipping = 64 - 47 = $17

Part B Answer: 6 pounds

Shipping = 5 + 2w

17 = 5 + 2w

12 = 2w

w = 6 pounds 📦

Part C Answer: $67

New shipping = 5 + 2.50(6) = 5 + 15 = $20

New total = 47 + 20 = $67

You would have paid $3 more! 💰
