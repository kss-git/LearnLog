Lesson 7: Statistical Graphs

Learning Objectives

  • Create and interpret different types of statistical graphs
  • Choose the appropriate graph type for different data
  • Analyze trends and patterns in visual data
  • Identify misleading graphs and common visualization errors

1. Types of Statistical Graphs

Choosing the Right Graph

  • Bar Graph: Comparing categories or discrete data
  • Line Graph: Showing trends over time
  • Pie Chart: Showing parts of a whole (percentages)
  • Histogram: Showing frequency distribution of continuous data
  • Scatter Plot: Showing relationship between two variables
  • Box Plot: Showing data distribution and outliers

Interpreting Line Graphs

  • Steep slope: Rapid change
  • Gentle slope: Slow change
  • Horizontal line: No change
  • Upward trend: Increase over time
  • Downward trend: Decrease over time

Histogram vs Bar Graph

  • Histogram: Shows continuous data, bars touch each other
  • Bar Graph: Shows categorical data, bars have gaps
  • Key difference: Histograms represent ranges; bar graphs represent distinct categories

Types of Correlation in Scatter Plots

  • Positive correlation: As one variable increases, the other increases
  • Negative correlation: As one variable increases, the other decreases
  • No correlation: No clear relationship between variables

6. Misleading Graphs

Common Ways Graphs Can Mislead

  • Truncated Y-axis: Starting the axis above zero to exaggerate differences
  • Inconsistent scales: Using different scales on the same graph
  • Missing labels: No units or source information
  • Cherry-picking data: Only showing data that supports a claim
  • 3D effects: Making comparisons difficult with unnecessary dimensions

Key Takeaways

  • Choose the right graph type based on your data and purpose
  • Always label axes clearly with units
  • Use appropriate scales - start bar graphs at zero for fair comparison
  • Line graphs show trends over time; scatter plots show relationships
  • Histograms have touching bars (continuous data); bar graphs have gaps (categories)
  • Be critical of graphs - check for misleading features
