When you tilt your smartphone to play a racing game, how does it know exactly how far you've tilted it? The answer lies in three magical ratios - sine, cosine, and tangent - that your phone's sensors use thousands of times per second to track orientation and motion!

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Define sine, cosine, and tangent ratios for right triangles
  • Use SOH-CAH-TOA to remember the three trig ratios
  • Calculate trig ratios given side lengths of a triangle
  • Find missing sides using trig ratios and angles

What You'll Learn

The three fundamental trigonometric ratios - sine, cosine, and tangent - are simply comparisons (ratios) between different sides of a right triangle. Each ratio has a specific meaning and use:

  • Sine (sin): Compares the opposite side to the hypotenuse
  • Cosine (cos): Compares the adjacent side to the hypotenuse
  • Tangent (tan): Compares the opposite side to the adjacent side

Think of these ratios as recipes that always give you the same result for a given angle, no matter how big or small the triangle is!

Why This Matters

These three ratios are the foundation of modern technology:

  • Smartphone Sensors: Accelerometers use sine and cosine to detect tilt and rotation
  • Video Games: Game engines use tangent to calculate viewing angles and perspective
  • Architecture: Engineers use these ratios to calculate roof slopes and structural angles
  • Navigation: Pilots and ships use trig ratios to calculate heading and altitude
  • Computer Graphics: Every 3D model rotation uses sine and cosine

Understanding these three ratios unlocks your ability to solve countless real-world problems!

Core Concept: The Three Trig Ratios

The Magic Formula: SOH-CAH-TOA

The easiest way to remember the three ratios is the mnemonic SOH-CAH-TOA:

SOH-CAH-TOA

SOH: Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse

CAH: Cosine = Adjacent / Hypotenuse

TOA: Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent

The Unit Circle: Understanding Sine and Cosine

The unit circle shows how sine and cosine work for any angle. A unit circle has radius = 1.

On the unit circle, for any angle θ: cos(θ) is the x-coordinate and sin(θ) is the y-coordinate!

Example 1: Calculating Trig Ratios

Problem: A right triangle has a hypotenuse of 13 cm, an opposite side of 5 cm, and an adjacent side of 12 cm. Find sin(θ), cos(θ), and tan(θ).

Solution:

Step 1: Identify the sides

  • Opposite = 5 cm
  • Adjacent = 12 cm
  • Hypotenuse = 13 cm

Step 2: Apply SOH-CAH-TOA

sin(θ) = Opposite / Hypotenuse = 5/13 ≈ 0.385

cos(θ) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse = 12/13 ≈ 0.923

tan(θ) = Opposite / Adjacent = 5/12 ≈ 0.417

Answers: sin(θ) = 5/13, cos(θ) = 12/13, tan(θ) = 5/12

Why this works: Each ratio compares specific sides. Notice that sin and cos both involve the hypotenuse, while tan doesn't use the hypotenuse at all!

Example 2: Finding a Missing Side

Problem: A ladder makes a 65° angle with the ground. If the ladder is 8 meters long, how high up the wall does it reach?

Solution:

  1. Draw and label the triangle:
    • Angle at ground = 65°
    • Hypotenuse (ladder) = 8 m
    • Opposite (height up wall) = ? (what we're finding)
  2. Choose the right ratio:

    We have the hypotenuse and want the opposite → Use SINE!

  3. Set up the equation:

    sin(65°) = opposite / hypotenuse

    sin(65°) = h / 8

  4. Solve for h:

    h = 8 × sin(65°)

    h = 8 × 0.906

    h ≈ 7.25 meters

Answer: The ladder reaches 7.25 meters up the wall

Quick Check: Test Your Understanding!

Question: A ramp has an angle of 20° with the ground and a length of 5 meters. Which trig ratio would you use to find the horizontal distance the ramp covers?

Answer: Cosine (CAH)

Explanation:

We have: angle (20°) and hypotenuse (5 m)

We want: adjacent (horizontal distance)

The ratio that connects hypotenuse and adjacent is COSINE!

cos(20°) = adjacent / 5

adjacent = 5 × cos(20°) ≈ 4.7 meters

Practice Questions

Question 1 (Easy): In the mnemonic SOH-CAH-TOA, what does the "O" in SOH stand for?

A) Over
B) Opposite
C) One
D) Obtuse

Answer: B) Opposite

SOH means Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse. The "O" stands for the opposite side.

Question 2 (Medium): A right triangle has an angle of 40°. The side opposite this angle is 6 cm, and the hypotenuse is 9.3 cm. Calculate sin(40°).

Answer: sin(40°) ≈ 0.645

Solution:

sin(40°) = opposite / hypotenuse

sin(40°) = 6 / 9.3

sin(40°) ≈ 0.645

Question 3 (Medium): Which trig ratio does NOT use the hypotenuse?

Answer: Tangent (TOA)

Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent. It's the only ratio that doesn't involve the hypotenuse!

Question 4 (Hard): A zip line makes a 25° angle with the horizontal. If the zip line is 200 meters long, how much vertical drop (height) does it have?

Answer: 84.5 meters

Solution:

Given: angle = 25°, hypotenuse = 200 m

Want: opposite (vertical drop)

Use: sin(25°) = opposite / 200

opposite = 200 × sin(25°)

opposite = 200 × 0.423

opposite ≈ 84.5 meters

Question 5 (Hard): Your smartphone's screen is tilted at 50° from horizontal. If the screen is 15 cm long (as the hypotenuse), what are the horizontal and vertical displacements?

Answers:

Horizontal displacement: 9.6 cm

Vertical displacement: 11.5 cm

Solution:

Horizontal = adjacent = hypotenuse × cos(50°)

Horizontal = 15 × 0.643 ≈ 9.6 cm

Vertical = opposite = hypotenuse × sin(50°)

Vertical = 15 × 0.766 ≈ 11.5 cm

This is exactly how your phone's accelerometer calculates tilt!

Quick Summary

  • SOH-CAH-TOA is the key to remembering the three trig ratios
  • Sine (sin) = Opposite / Hypotenuse
  • Cosine (cos) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
  • Tangent (tan) = Opposite / Adjacent
  • Use these ratios to find missing sides when you know an angle
  • Sin and cos both use the hypotenuse; tan doesn't

Real-World Connection: Smartphone Accelerometers

Every modern smartphone contains an accelerometer - a tiny chip that detects orientation and motion. Here's how it uses trigonometry:

How it works:

  • Sensing gravity: The accelerometer measures gravity's pull in three directions (x, y, z)
  • Calculating angles: Using sine and cosine, it determines the phone's tilt angles
  • Rotation detection: When you rotate your phone, sin and cos values change
  • Gaming applications: Racing games use these angles to steer vehicles

The math:

If gravity pulls with force G at angle θ from vertical:

  • Vertical component = G × cos(θ)
  • Horizontal component = G × sin(θ)

The phone measures these components thousands of times per second, using sine and cosine to calculate the exact tilt angle. This is why games respond instantly when you tilt your device!

Challenge Problem

The Drone Photography Problem:

A photographer is using a drone to take aerial photos. The drone hovers at a constant height and needs to photograph a building from a 35° angle of depression (looking down from horizontal).

Part A: If the drone is 120 meters above the ground, how far horizontally is the drone from the building?

Part B: What is the straight-line distance from the drone to the base of the building?

Part C: If the photographer wants to capture the shot from a 45° angle instead, how far should the drone move horizontally (keeping the same height)?

Hint for Part A: The angle of depression from the drone creates a triangle where the height (120m) is the opposite side. Think about which trig ratio connects opposite and adjacent!

Hint for Part C: At 45°, there's something special about the opposite and adjacent sides...

Part A Answer: 171.3 meters

Solution:

Given: opposite (height) = 120 m, angle = 35°

Want: adjacent (horizontal distance)

tan(35°) = opposite / adjacent

tan(35°) = 120 / adjacent

adjacent = 120 / tan(35°)

adjacent = 120 / 0.700 ≈ 171.3 meters

Part B Answer: 209.1 meters

Solution:

We need the hypotenuse (straight-line distance)

sin(35°) = opposite / hypotenuse

sin(35°) = 120 / hypotenuse

hypotenuse = 120 / sin(35°)

hypotenuse = 120 / 0.574 ≈ 209.1 meters

Part C Answer: Move 51.3 meters closer

Solution:

At 45°: tan(45°) = 1 (special property!)

tan(45°) = 120 / adjacent

1 = 120 / adjacent

adjacent = 120 meters

Original distance was 171.3 m, new distance is 120 m

Change = 171.3 - 120 = 51.3 meters closer

Special note: At 45°, the opposite and adjacent sides are equal!
