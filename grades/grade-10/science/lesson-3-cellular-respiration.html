Lesson 3: Cellular Respiration

How Your Cells Turn Food Into Energy

Home Grade 10 Science

Introduction

Every time you breathe, move, think, or even blink, you're using energy. But where does this energy come from? It's not from the food you eat directly - it comes from ATP (adenosine triphosphate), the universal energy currency of cells. Cellular respiration is the process that converts the energy in food into ATP that your body can actually use.

The Simple Version: You eat food → Your cells break it down → Energy is released and stored in ATP → ATP powers everything you do!

The Cellular Respiration Equation

C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ATP Energy

Glucose + Oxygen → Carbon Dioxide + Water + Energy

Breaking it down:

  • Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆): Sugar from the food you eat
  • Oxygen (O₂): What you breathe in
  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂): Waste gas you breathe out
  • Water (H₂O): Waste product
  • ATP: Energy your cells can use immediately!

Inside the Mitochondrion: The Powerhouse

Cellular respiration happens mainly inside mitochondria. Let's look at this amazing organelle!

Why are cristae (folds) important? The inner membrane is highly folded to increase surface area. More surface area = more space for ATP production = more energy! Athletes have MORE cristae in their mitochondria than sedentary people.

The Three Stages of Cellular Respiration

Stage 1: Glycolysis (Glucose Splitting)

Where: Cytoplasm (outside mitochondria)

What happens: One glucose molecule (6 carbons) breaks into TWO pyruvate molecules (3 carbons each)

Energy produced: 2 ATP (net gain)

No oxygen needed! This is anaerobic (can happen without oxygen)

Glucose (6C) → 2 Pyruvate (3C each) + 2 ATP + 2 NADH

Stage 2: Krebs Cycle (Citric Acid Cycle)

Where: Mitochondrial matrix

What happens: Pyruvate is completely broken down, releasing CO₂

Energy produced: 2 ATP + energy carriers (NADH and FADH₂)

Requires oxygen! This is aerobic

2 Pyruvate → 6 CO₂ + 2 ATP + 8 NADH + 2 FADH₂

The CO₂ you breathe out? It comes from this step!

Stage 3: Electron Transport Chain (ETC)

Where: Inner mitochondrial membrane (cristae)

What happens: Energy carriers (NADH, FADH₂) release electrons that flow through proteins, creating a "proton pump" that generates LOTS of ATP

Energy produced: 32-34 ATP (the BIG payoff!)

Requires oxygen! Oxygen is the final electron acceptor, forming water

NADH + FADH₂ + O₂ → ~34 ATP + H₂O

This is why you need to breathe! Without oxygen, the ETC stops, and you only get 2 ATP per glucose instead of 36-38. That's a 95% energy loss!

Total Energy Scorecard

Stage Location ATP Produced
Glycolysis Cytoplasm 2 ATP
Krebs Cycle Matrix 2 ATP
Electron Transport Chain Inner Membrane 32-34 ATP
TOTAL - 36-38 ATP

Cellular Respiration and Exercise

💪 Aerobic Exercise (With Oxygen)

Examples: Jogging, swimming, cycling at steady pace

What happens: You breathe fast enough to supply oxygen → Full cellular respiration → Maximum ATP production (36-38 per glucose)

Result: Sustained energy, can exercise for long periods

Waste products: CO₂ (breathed out) and H₂O (sweat)

🏃‍♂️ Anaerobic Exercise (Without Enough Oxygen)

Examples: Sprinting, weightlifting, intense bursts

What happens: Muscles need energy FASTER than oxygen can be delivered → Glycolysis only → Produces lactic acid

Energy produced: Only 2 ATP per glucose (much less!)

Result: Quick energy but muscles fatigue fast

The "burn" you feel? That's lactic acid buildup in your muscles!

Glucose → 2 Pyruvate → 2 Lactic Acid + 2 ATP

😮‍💨 Why You Keep Breathing Hard After Exercise

After intense exercise, you have "oxygen debt." Your body needs extra oxygen to:

  • Convert lactic acid back to glucose (in the liver)
  • Restore ATP and energy carrier molecules
  • Return oxygen levels in blood and muscles to normal

This is why you keep breathing heavily even after you stop running!

Food Energy and Cellular Respiration

🍎 Different Foods, Same Process

Your body can convert different macronutrients into glucose or directly into the Krebs Cycle:

  • Carbohydrates (bread, pasta, fruit): Broken down into glucose → enters glycolysis directly. FASTEST energy source!
  • Fats (oils, butter, nuts): Broken down into fatty acids → enters Krebs Cycle. MOST energy per gram (9 calories vs. 4 for carbs). Great for endurance!
  • Proteins (meat, beans, eggs): Broken down into amino acids → can enter Krebs Cycle. Usually used for building muscle, not energy (unless you're starving)

Sports Nutrition Tip: Carbs for quick energy (pre-game meal), fats for sustained endurance (long hikes), and proteins for muscle recovery (post-workout)!

Why Cellular Respiration Matters

🧠 Brain Power

Your brain uses 20% of your body's oxygen and glucose! When blood sugar drops, you feel tired and can't think clearly. That's why eating breakfast improves focus and test performance.

❤️ Heart & Circulation

Your heart beats 100,000 times per day, powered entirely by ATP from cellular respiration. Heart muscle cells are PACKED with mitochondria!

🏋️ Athletic Performance

Training increases mitochondria in muscle cells (called "mitochondrial biogenesis"). More mitochondria = more ATP = better endurance and strength!

🔬 Amazing Fact!

Every day, your body produces and uses roughly your own body weight in ATP! A 70 kg person makes about 70 kg of ATP daily. But ATP is constantly recycled - each ATP molecule is created, used, and remade about 500-700 times per day. Talk about efficient recycling!
