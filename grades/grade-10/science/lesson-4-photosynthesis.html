Lesson 4: Photosynthesis

How Plants Capture Sunlight and Create Life's Energy

Home Grade 10 Science

Introduction

Every breath you take, every meal you eat, and every bit of energy you use can be traced back to one source: the sun. But you can't eat sunlight! That's where plants come in. Through photosynthesis, plants capture light energy and convert it into chemical energy (glucose) that powers nearly all life on Earth - including you!

Think About It: The energy that powers your sprint during PE class originally came from the sun, captured by plants, eaten by you or an animal you ate, and converted to ATP through cellular respiration. You are solar-powered!

The Photosynthesis Equation

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Carbon Dioxide + Water + Sunlight → Glucose + Oxygen

Notice Something Familiar?

Photosynthesis: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

Cellular Respiration: C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ATP

They're OPPOSITE processes! Plants make the glucose and oxygen that animals (like you) need, and animals make the CO₂ and water that plants need. It's a perfect cycle!

Inside the Chloroplast: The Solar Panel of Cells

Photosynthesis happens in chloroplasts - green organelles found in plant cells (and algae). Let's explore this amazing structure!

Key Structures:

  • Thylakoids: Disc-shaped membrane sacs where light reactions occur
  • Grana (plural) / Granum (singular): Stacks of thylakoids, like stacked pancakes
  • Stroma: Fluid-filled space surrounding thylakoids where the Calvin Cycle occurs
  • Chlorophyll: Green pigment embedded in thylakoid membranes that captures light energy

The Two Stages of Photosynthesis

☀️ Stage 1: Light-Dependent Reactions (Light Reactions)

Where: Thylakoid membranes

Requires: Sunlight, water (H₂O)

What happens:

  1. Chlorophyll absorbs light energy
  2. Water molecules are split (H₂O → H⁺ + O)
  3. Oxygen (O₂) is released as a waste product - this is the oxygen you breathe!
  4. Energy is stored in energy carrier molecules (ATP and NADPH)

Light + H₂O → O₂ + ATP + NADPH

Fun Fact: All the oxygen in Earth's atmosphere came from photosynthesis! Before plants evolved, there was almost no O₂ to breathe.

🔄 Stage 2: Light-Independent Reactions (Calvin Cycle)

Where: Stroma (fluid inside chloroplast)

Requires: CO₂, ATP and NADPH (from light reactions)

What happens:

  1. CO₂ from the air is "fixed" (captured)
  2. Using energy from ATP and NADPH, CO₂ is converted into glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆)
  3. Glucose is used by the plant for energy or stored as starch

6 CO₂ + ATP + NADPH → C₆H₁₂O₆ (glucose)

Why "Light-Independent"? This cycle doesn't need light directly, BUT it needs the ATP and NADPH made during the light reactions. So it still depends on light indirectly!

Comparing Photosynthesis and Cellular Respiration

Feature Photosynthesis Cellular Respiration
Organisms Plants, algae, some bacteria All living organisms
Location Chloroplasts Mitochondria (mostly)
Energy Source Sunlight Glucose
Inputs CO₂ + H₂O + Light C₆H₁₂O₆ + O₂
Outputs C₆H₁₂O₆ + O₂ CO₂ + H₂O + ATP
Purpose Store energy (builds glucose) Release energy (breaks down glucose)
Type of Process Endergonic (requires energy input) Exergonic (releases energy)

The Big Picture: Photosynthesis stores sun energy in glucose. Cellular respiration releases that stored energy as ATP. Together, they form a continuous energy cycle that powers all life on Earth!

Photosynthesis and Your Food

🥗 Plants: Direct Solar Energy

When you eat fruits, vegetables, grains, or nuts, you're consuming glucose that plants made through photosynthesis. The energy in that apple or piece of bread originally came from the sun!

🍔 Animals: Indirect Solar Energy

When you eat meat, chicken, or fish, you're eating an animal that ate plants (or ate another animal that ate plants). The energy STILL came from photosynthesis, just with extra steps!

Energy Flow:

Sun → Plants (photosynthesis) → Animals → You → Cellular Respiration → ATP → Movement, thinking, growth!

Factors Affecting Photosynthesis Rate

☀️ Light Intensity

Effect: More light = faster photosynthesis (up to a point)

Real example: Plants grow faster in bright sunlight than in shade. This is why gardens need full sun!

💨 CO₂ Concentration

Effect: More CO₂ = faster photosynthesis

Greenhouse application: Commercial greenhouses pump in extra CO₂ to boost plant growth!

🌡️ Temperature

Effect: Optimal temperature range (usually 25-35°C for most plants)

Why: Enzymes that run photosynthesis work best at specific temperatures. Too cold = slow reactions. Too hot = enzymes denature (stop working)

💧 Water Availability

Effect: Sufficient water needed for light reactions

Real example: During drought, plants close their stomata (pores) to save water, but this also blocks CO₂ entry, slowing photosynthesis

Why Photosynthesis Matters

🌍 Life Support System

Photosynthesis produces virtually ALL the oxygen on Earth. Without it, animals couldn't breathe, and the atmosphere would be toxic!

🍎 Food Supply

95% of all food comes directly or indirectly from photosynthesis. No photosynthesis = no food chain = no life as we know it.

🌡️ Climate Regulation

Plants absorb CO₂ (a greenhouse gas) during photosynthesis, helping regulate Earth's temperature. Deforestation reduces this CO₂ absorption, contributing to climate change.

⛽ Fossil Fuels

Coal, oil, and natural gas are ancient plants that stored sun energy through photosynthesis millions of years ago. When we burn them, we're releasing that ancient solar energy!

The Carbon-Oxygen Cycle

This cycle has been running for billions of years, constantly recycling carbon and oxygen between plants and animals. It's one of nature's most perfect partnerships!

🔬 Mind-Blowing Facts!

  • Photosynthesis produces about 170 billion tons of glucose every year on Earth!
  • Ocean algae and phytoplankton produce 50-80% of Earth's oxygen - not trees!
  • It takes about 6 trees to offset one person's annual CO₂ emissions through photosynthesis.
  • A single large tree can produce enough oxygen for two people for an entire year!
